The Story of Endrobe

Endrobe was founded on the belief that education should be as unique as the individual pursuing it. Conceived as a flexible certification agency, it operates on a "learning objective-first" model, prioritizing the specific goals and needs of students across Pakistan.

The Journey of Duaa Mumtaz

I've always been a person with more than one interests. People used to say that the one who has multiple interests can't excel in anything. I've spent my life proving that wrong. From being the only Pakistani in international MUNs, i used to feel odd one out but I've always learnt from my experience. From debating at international MUNs to shadowing doctors on clinical rounds, and from preparing for medical entrance exams to teaching myself "how to code", I've learned that passion is the only map you need in your life. In many countries, especially in Pakistan, students are often told to bury their other interests the moment they start preparing for medical school. I find it completely wrong. I built this platform because I wanted to create a space where students, regardless of their age, grade or background, can freely explore a mix of majors, extracurriculars, languages, and gaming. These choices shouldn't be reserved for university students; hey are the bare minimum every learner deserves. This platform is my answer to that need.

Beyond my academic interests, I am deeply invested in humanitarian work through "ParhoPakistan," an initiative I founded to provide books and stationery to underprivileged students. I believe everyone deserves a fair chance to learn, which is why I started teaching underprivileged students free of cost. I have actively integrated my skills across both my studies and my extracurriculars. I started writing novels at a very young age; this experience led me to contribute to medical researches and academic articles. Driven by a great passion for human wellness, I also co-founded 'Clarytin,' a mental health awareness initiative."
